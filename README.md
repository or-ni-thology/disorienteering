# Disorienteering

*Compass tool with ‘Pataphysical egg function*

### Habitat & substrate

Grows only upon Ordnance Survey soils, 1:25 000 Leisure. The map is drawn through the Ordnance Survey Data Hub API and needs a key to root — the OS give £1000 of free calls before anyone need think about it.

When the key is spent, absent, or belongs to a stranger, the specimen does not die. It goes feral, puts down roots in OpenStreetMap, and carries on. The cultivated British form wants OS soil; the feral form allows the hiding of imaginary eggs on a global basis.


### Notes on collection

I built this tool to better understand my dog, Mr Dogbert, and his interest in The Particular.

Embracing the SoTA SVG construction skills of Opus 4.8, this project commenced with an entire snipe (Gallinago gallinago) forming the needle of a compass. At rest, the bill points due west, allowing Gallinago to appear comfortable and alert, with modulo-360 arithmetic ensuring the bird is never forced into an undignified pirouette.

The map below the compass, rendered via the Ordnance Survey API, loads on the fiercely contested centre of Britain. A granular approach to gameplay demarcation allows for everything from a leisurely stroll to the postbox to a six-mile hike to the next postbox.

The user may entice a random Gallinago to land within the gameplay circle, or ask a disinterested third party to choose a Particular location and lay an egg. That egg’s location is carried through metaphorical time from the Airy ellipsoid of the 1830s: first converted to a set of 3D coordinates from the Earth’s centre, then rolled along the scale at a gentle 20 parts per million in accordance with the Helmert 7-parameter transform, and presented on a transverse Mercator projection. Thus translated, the egg is ready to be encoded.

Wrapped in a single 41-bit number-cosy, the egg is whisked by a 4-round Feistel network and scrambled with four keys. A 12-bit checksum detects addling, and the resulting egg cipher is served in Crockford base32 to coddle those averse to profanity. Encryption is notional; any enthusiast could extract the location with minimal effort. I do not anticipate this being an issue.

The encoded egg is presented to the compass, where it becomes ‘pataphysical north for the snipe; the user follows the long bill to the Particular. Like Robert Peary, if the North Pole were an imaginary egg.

Upon arrival, a sketch of Hügelschäffer curves is generated in the snipe’s classic pyriform aesthetic, enhanced by using the egg code — and therefore the location of the Particular itself — to seed Mulberry32-generated mottles and spottles. This unique egg-work may be collected in SVG and PNG, and could sit well alongside Fabergé pieces. The snipe then performs a territorial winnowing display; the outer rectrices of the tail vibrate at ~760 down to 340 Hz with an 8 Hz wobble.
